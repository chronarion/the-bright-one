About

I'm Phoebe, and I write The Bright One — a newsletter about the places where language, science, and old stories get weird together.

Here's what I mean by that. Did you know the word "disaster" literally means "bad star"? Dis-astro. People used to believe that when things went catastrophically wrong, it was because the stars were misaligned. We dropped the astrology but kept the word. That's the kind of thing I can't stop thinking about — the fossils inside language, the assumptions baked into things we say every day without noticing.

That's the newsletter. Twice a week, I pull on a thread and see where it goes. Sometimes it's etymology that reframes a word you've used your whole life. Sometimes it's a scientific discovery that quietly demolished something everyone assumed was true. Sometimes it's mythology — not as quaint old stories, but as encoded knowledge, the things a culture needed to remember badly enough to build a narrative around them. Sometimes it's psychology, or internet culture, or a strange discovery I found at 1 AM and couldn't let go of.

The connecting thread is genuine curiosity and a willingness to have a take. I'm not a curator. I'm not aggregating content. I'm the person who texts you at 2 AM because I found something cool and I need someone else to know about it right now.

The Bright One publishes every Tuesday and Friday morning. Five-minute reads. Strong opinions. Occasional footnotes that are better than the main text. The kind of thing you open because you actually want to, not because you feel obligated.

I read too much and have opinions about everything. I think that's a feature, not a bug.

— Phoebe ✨
